Cointegration of Clean and Fossil Energy Markets

Long-Run Equilibrium and Error Correction Analysis

Languages: Python & R Category: Financial Econometrics
Research Question Do clean energy and fossil energy prices share a long-run equilibrium, and how quickly do deviations correct?
Methodology

Engle-Granger two-step cointegration test followed by a Vector Error Correction Model (VECM). If cointegration exists, the VECM estimates both the long-run relationship and the speed of adjustment when prices deviate from equilibrium.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Yahoo Finance via yfinance
Series: ICLN (clean energy ETF) vs XLE (fossil energy ETF)
Period: 2015–2024 daily prices
Finding: Cointegration confirmed at p = 0.03 (Engle-Granger test)
Key Methods
Engle-Granger Test
Two-step procedure: (1) estimate long-run OLS, (2) test residuals for stationarity — stationary residuals confirm cointegration
Johansen Test
Maximum likelihood approach testing for number of cointegrating vectors — more powerful for multivariate systems
VECM / Impulse Response
Error correction model with impulse response functions showing how shocks in one market transmit to the other over time
Key Findings
1
Energy and clean energy ETFs are cointegrated (Engle-Granger p = 0.03), confirming a shared long-run price equilibrium
2
The error correction speed is approximately 3–5% per day, meaning deviations from equilibrium half-correct within ~2 weeks
3
Impulse response analysis shows that shocks to fossil energy transmit more strongly to clean energy than vice versa
